# Phase 5 Role Style Policy Examples

Phase 5 role style policies are prompt-level guidance only. They change preferred wording and emphasis, not content selection and not truth constraints.

## Backend

- Preferred emphasis: APIs, services, reliability, scalability, data flow
- Vocabulary cluster: `apis`, `services`, `backend`, `reliability`, `scalability`
- Discouraged wording: UI-first phrasing, vague ownership language

## Frontend

- Preferred emphasis: interfaces, UX, accessibility, performance, design systems
- Vocabulary cluster: `interfaces`, `frontend`, `ux`, `accessibility`, `design systems`
- Discouraged wording: backend infrastructure framing, generic polish language

## Full-Stack

- Preferred emphasis: balanced frontend and backend contribution, integration
- Vocabulary cluster: `full-stack`, `frontend`, `backend`, `apis`, `interfaces`
- Discouraged wording: unsupported breadth or architecture claims

## DevOps / Platform

- Preferred emphasis: automation, infrastructure, CI/CD, deployment, observability, reliability
- Vocabulary cluster: `automation`, `infrastructure`, `ci/cd`, `deployment`, `observability`
- Discouraged wording: product-feature framing, unsupported org-wide ownership

## Data / Analytics

- Preferred emphasis: pipelines, modeling, analysis, experimentation, insights
- Vocabulary cluster: `pipelines`, `etl`, `modeling`, `analysis`, `insights`
- Discouraged wording: unsupported ML specialization or strategy inflation

## Product

- Preferred emphasis: prioritization, roadmap, user impact, cross-functional delivery
- Vocabulary cluster: `roadmap`, `prioritization`, `outcomes`, `user impact`, `cross-functional`
- Discouraged wording: unsupported technical specialization, vision fluff

## Engineering Management / Leadership

- Preferred emphasis: leadership, team development, delivery, stakeholder alignment
- Vocabulary cluster: `leadership`, `team development`, `delivery`, `stakeholder alignment`, `execution`
- Discouraged wording: heroic IC framing, unsupported org-scale leadership claims

## Neutral Fallback

- Used for unsupported or unknown role families
- Keeps guidance factual, concise, and non-specialized
- Never relaxes rewrite-policy guardrails
